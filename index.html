I am told a whippersnapper jokingly equates to a millenial. By definition a whippersnapper is: "a young person who annoys older people by being very confident and acting like someone important (Merriam-Webster)." It is a word often used to describe the millenials taking over Silicon Valley, but I'd like to think we are just a generation shaped by the ones before us. I'd like to think that a whippersnapper is anyone with creative solutions to real world problems - albeit young and/or inexperienced. Therefore, let it be re-written, that a whippersnapper is a bold individual who is full of brillant ideas to improve the world around them.
